# Adoption Strategy

How could GHS move from a GitHub repository to a global standard? Four levels of adoption would need to align.

## 1. Political Level: UN General Assembly

The UN is the only institution capable of creating worldwide acceptance.

**Historical precedent**: In the 1920s and 1930s, the League of Nations intensively debated the International Fixed Calendar (IFC). It failed primarily due to religious opposition to "World Days" that break the weekly cycle — a problem GHS explicitly solves by using the Aurora Week instead.

**The process**: A member state would need to introduce a resolution draft. The UN would convene a "World Calendar Conference." GHS's key selling point: *"We disturb neither the Sabbath, nor Sunday, nor Friday. We simply make the year more efficient and nature-aligned."*

## 2. Technical Level: ISO

While the UN handles politics, ISO handles technology.

- **ISO 8601** is the current global standard for date/time formats (YYYY-MM-DD).
- A new standard (e.g., ISO 8601:2028-GHS) would need to define how computers interpret "23 Luna" or "an Aurora Week."
- Every database, flight plan, banking system, and operating system worldwide would need updates.

## 3. Scientific Level: BIPM & IAU

- **BIPM** (International Bureau of Weights and Measures): Custodians of UTC. They would need to define precisely how the equinox is measured to fix New Year's Day.
- **IAU** (International Astronomical Union): They would validate the Aurora Algorithm's long-term stability to ensure the calendar remains accurate over millennia.

## 4. Economic Level: G20 & Financial Markets

This is where the strongest resistance lies.

**Quarterly reporting**: The global economy runs on 3-month quarters. 13 months don't divide by 4. GHS addresses this with week-based quarters (4 × 13 weeks = 4 × 91 days), but every accounting system would need adaptation.

**Fiscal year**: A year beginning in March would disrupt the tax year of most Western nations. The G20 would likely need a 10–20 year transition period.

## Southern Hemisphere Diplomacy

GHS starts the year in "spring" (equinox). In Australia, South Africa, and Brazil, March is autumn. Two options:

1. Accept that "March" in the southern hemisphere marks an autumnal beginning — the equinox is still a real astronomical event, just with reversed seasons.
2. Use abstract month names that don't imply a specific season.

The existing month names are retained because they are globally familiar, even if their seasonal connotation is hemisphere-dependent.

## Dual-Date Transition Phase

No system switches overnight. A parallel display period is needed (like price tags during the Euro introduction):

```
GHS 12026.01.09 @875  (29 Mar 2026, 21:00 UTC)
```

An official **GHS prefix** prevents confusion between old and new date formats during the transition.

## The Religious Advantage

Unlike the IFC, GHS never introduces a "day outside the week." The 7-day Sabbath/Sunday/Friday cycle continues unbroken. This removes the single most powerful objection that killed calendar reform in the 20th century.
