# Physics Implications of @Beats

If @Beats replaced SI seconds as the fundamental unit of time in everyday life, the ripple effects on physics and engineering would be enormous.

## Speed Units

Currently we express speed in km/h or m/s. With beats:

- **Kilometers per beat (km/@)**: At 120 km/h, you travel ~2.88 km per beat.
- **Meters per centibeat (m/cB)**: 120 km/h ≈ 28.8 m/cB — a more intuitive scale for vehicle speeds.

The dashboard would read in km/@ or m/cB instead of km/h.

## Gravitational Acceleration

The familiar constant g = 9.81 m/s² would become:

```
g ≈ 7.31 m/cB²
```

Every physics formula containing time would need conversion. Every textbook would need reprinting.

## The Scale of Impact

This isn't just cosmetic. The SI second appears in:
- Newton's laws (F = ma, where a is m/s²)
- Energy (Joules = kg·m²/s²)
- Electrical units (Ampere = Coulombs/second)
- Radiation dosimetry
- GPS satellite calculations

Redefining the time unit cascades through the entire system of measurement.

## Two Paths Forward

### Path A: The "Layer" Approach (Realistic)

Keep the SI second for science and technology. Use @Beats only for social/civil time. Computers calculate in seconds internally but display beats to users.

- **Advantage**: No disruption to physics, engineering, GPS, or quantum computing
- **Disadvantage**: Two parallel time systems — one for machines, one for humans

### Path B: The "Total Revolution" (Manifesto Vision)

Redefine time as a fraction of the day. Everything becomes decimal.

- **Advantage**: Maximum simplicity — time arithmetic works like money
- **Disadvantage**: Requires rebooting spaceflight, GPS satellites, and quantum physics

## Recommendation

Path A is the pragmatic choice for adoption. The SI second is defined by caesium-133 atomic oscillations and underpins too much critical infrastructure to replace.

Path B remains a philosophical north star — the "pure" vision of what a fully decimal civilization could look like.
