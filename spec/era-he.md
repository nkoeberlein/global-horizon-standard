# Human Era (HE) Specification

## 1. Definition

The Global Horizon Standard uses the **Human Era (HE)** as its epoch. The conversion is simple:

```
Year HE = Gregorian Year + 10,000
```

The current year (2026 CE) is **12,026 HE**.

## 2. Rationale

The Gregorian calendar anchors history to a single religious event, splitting all of human civilization into "BC" and "AD". This creates two fundamental problems:

1. **Reverse counting**: Events before the epoch must be counted backwards, making intuitive chronological reasoning difficult. "Was 1500 BC before or after 2000 BC?" requires mental gymnastics.

2. **Cultural exclusion**: Billions of people do not share the religious event at year zero. A universal standard should not privilege one tradition over all others.

The Human Era solves both problems by adding 10,000 years, placing the epoch at approximately **10,000 BCE** — the dawn of the Neolithic Revolution.

## 3. Historical Justification

Around 12,000 years ago, humanity crossed a threshold:

- **Göbekli Tepe** (~9500 BCE / ~500 HE): The oldest known monumental structure, built by hunter-gatherers in present-day Turkey. Evidence that complex civilization predates agriculture.
- **The Neolithic Revolution**: The transition from nomadic hunting to settled agriculture, animal domestication, and permanent villages.
- **The first cities**: Jericho, Çatalhöyük, and other early settlements emerged in this era.

By setting year 1 HE at this threshold, the entire arc of human civilization — from the first temples to the space age — fits into a single, continuous, positive timeline.

## 4. Academic Reference

The Human Era calendar was proposed by **Cesare Emiliani** in 1993 (Nature, Vol. 366). Emiliani, a geologist and micropaleontologist, argued that a calendar starting at the approximate beginning of the Holocene epoch would better serve scientific and historical discourse.

## 5. Practical Impact

| Event                    | Gregorian      | Human Era       |
|--------------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Göbekli Tepe             | ~9500 BC       | ~500 HE         |
| Great Pyramid of Giza    | ~2500 BC       | ~7,500 HE       |
| Founding of Rome         | 753 BC         | 9,247 HE        |
| Industrial Revolution    | ~1760 AD       | 11,760 HE       |
| Moon Landing             | 1969 AD        | 11,969 HE       |
| Today                    | 2026 AD        | 12,026 HE       |

Every event in human history has a positive year number. No reverse counting. One continuous story.

## 6. Notation

The year is written as a five-digit number with a dot separator for readability:

- **12.026 HE** (formal)
- **12026** (compact / machine-readable)
